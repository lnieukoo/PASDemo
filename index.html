Design and Development!

The design and technology choices behind the Pet Aid System

How it was designed...

The mobile first approach

  • Simple - allows for a neat UI and friendly user experience on mobile devices
  • Logical - a logical navigational experience
  • Modern - based on modern UI/UX research
  • Efficient - a bottom navigation panel for a faster user experience

The technologies behind the scenes...

A "reactive" front end

  • React - an efficient front end rendering framework
  • Material UI - a CSS framework for mobile friendly styling
  • Firebase Database - a real time back end database hosted by google
  • Firebase Authentication - an authentication service hosted by google
  • HTML 5 Geolocation - a service used to accurately estimate a users location

Current Stories

Stories that can be fulfilled

The newcomer

The Smith family have just relocated from Perth to Melbourne with their furry friend. They are new to the area and wish to explore the cafes of Melbourne on the weekend but are unsure whether they can take their pet. Mr Smith decides to jump onto the Pet Aid System, navigate to the food section in the bottom navigation panel, clicking on cafes and searching for pet friendly cafes nearby. The Smith family can now be assured their pet will be welcome into the cafe.

The business owner

Stephanie decides she wants to start her own pet sitting business but doesn't have the time or money to have a website developed. Stephanie can now make use of the Pet Aid System to generate business. She is able to login to the Pet Aid System via googles authentication mechanism, navigate to the create activity page and create a pet sitters activity. The web administrator can then contact Stephanie and approve or deny the activity created and potentially generate initial business for Stephanie.

The traveller

Jason and his wife love travelling up north and down south in Western Australia. They are excited to travel but are worried that they may need to leave their dog Billy at home due to the limitations and restrictions of pet friendly venues. Jason is now able to do searches for cafes, vets, hiking trails, beaches and much more, to ensure that Billy will also be able to get the most out of his holiday!

Future Stories

Stories for future development

Rate my business

Jessica has recently opened up a new cafe and is certain that her customers are extremely happy with her pet friendly venue. Jessica believes that if customers were able to leave reviews about her cafe, she would generate more business! Jessica's cafe is shown in the Pet Aid System and will be able to be reviewed in the near future, potentially with a high rating which will ultimately generate her more business.

The social pet

Zoro is a kelpie who loves playing with other dogs of his breed. Unfortunately, at the park nearby there are no other kelpies! Zoro's owner has decided to check online to see where there are other kelpie dog groups. The Pet Aid System, in the near future, will have it's events section developed which will allow for the quick and easy searching of events to suit the owners pet!

Take me there

Carla has a pomeranian who really needs grooming! She has found a few places to take her pet on the Pet Aid System but they are all too far to walk. Unfortunately Carla doesn't have a car and is more likely to travel by Uber. Uber is now offering pet friendly transport and the Pet Aid System hopes to interact directly with the Uber API to allow unity between the Pet Aid System and Uber, creating an extremely user and travel friendly experience!
